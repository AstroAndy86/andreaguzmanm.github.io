Hola! I am Andrea!

I am a second-year PhD student in Astrophysics at the University of Bern.

In November 2018 I joined the Center for Space and Habitability (CSH) of the University of Bern, to work under the mentorship of Prof. Dr. Kevin Heng and Prof. Dr. Christoph Mordasini. Originally a mathematician, I received a full scholarship from the European Union for a two-year Astrophysics joint master’s programme under the Erasmus Mundus network called Astromundus. I graduated in September of 2018 with a joint MSc degree from each university I attended: University of Innsbruck, Austria, University of Rome Tor Vergata, Italy and University of Göttingen, Germany, where I wrote my thesis on the Chromatic Rossiter McLaughlin effect as a way to study the atmosphere of the exoplanet HD 189733b using data from the CARMENES spectrograph.

Currently my research lies in the intersection between exoplanet atmospheres, planet interiors and machine learning and aims at understanding how we can couple our knowledge about the interior and the atmosphere of exoplanets.

I love languages: I speak Spanish, English, Italian and Portuguese and currently I'm learning French and a bit of German.

Research

Recently I am interested in helping the exoplanet community is studying the balance between investments of telescope time and scientific yield of the upcoming JWST mission.

Information content of warm Neptunes using Machine Learning

We explore an interesting and novel application of the random forest machine learning technique applied to the atmospheric retrieval of simulated JWST observations for mini-Neptunes. The goal of the study is to evaluate the information content of various JWST instrument modes, highlighting differing sensitivities to various exoplanet atmospheric parameters via unique byproducts of the random forest technique.

ESP Open Opacity Database

We need opacities of atoms and molecules in all sort of (exo)planetary atmospheric calculations! But computing opacities is a very difficult task that starts with line lists that have very large number of transitions. It takes an incredible amount of computing time to do so! We do not want to “reinvent the wheel” for this tedious step of transforming line list data into opacities for multiple points in T and P every time, therefore we are creating a publicly available opacity database for the exoplanet community.
